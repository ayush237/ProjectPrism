### Draft 1: The "Virality" Framework (Placeholder: Distributed Systems)

Everyone wants to build microservices. Almost no one actually needs them.

We’ve started treating distributed systems like a default architecture instead of a scaling optimization. If your engineering team is under 20 people and your traffic is predictable, you don't have a scaling problem. You have a domain boundary problem.

Splitting a tightly coupled monolith into 15 tightly coupled microservices doesn't fix your architecture. It just replaces local function calls with network latency. 

Instead of a single codebase you can debug locally, you now have to manage:
• Partial network failures
• Distributed tracing overhead
• Eventual consistency across disjointed databases
• Complex CI/CD pipelines just to ship a minor feature

The pragmatic approach: Start with a modular monolith. Enforce strict domain boundaries in your code, not over the network. 

Extract a service only when a specific module requires independent scaling, different hardware profiles, or isolated deployment cycles.
